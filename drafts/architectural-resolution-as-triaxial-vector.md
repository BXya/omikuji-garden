# Architectural Resolution as Triaxial Vector

Resolution treated as a single dial — more detail, more precision, more "completion" — is a weak critical instrument. It evaluates what a building looks like rather than what it does. A scalar model cannot accommodate the fact that Lebbeus Woods and Mies van der Rohe occupy the same "low resolution" position in conventional discourse, yet are doing entirely different things.

Three axes, each independently variable: **Formal** (Articulated ↔ Diffuse, observable as joint density per m²), **Structural** (Legible ↔ Alegible, observable as legibility of the force path), and **Semantic** (Charged ↔ Mute, observable as active connotative layers).

Combined, the three axes yield eight octants — from A–L–C, the Total Construct (Morphosis), and A–L–M, the Technical Sublime (Nervi), through D–L–C, the Ethical Field (Frei Otto), and D–L–M, Structural Silence (Mies), to D–A–C, Speculative Rupture (Lebbeus Woods), and D–A–M, Raw Matter.

The critical proof is D–A–C. If resolution were scalar, Woods and Mies occupy the same zone. They do not. Mies: D–L–M. Woods: D–A–C. The three axes are genuinely independent. That independence is the framework's original contribution.
